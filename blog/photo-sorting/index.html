Photo Sorting

May 16, 2021

The year is 2004. You acquire a digital camera which allows you to take pictures whenever and wherever you please, limited only by your 512MB SD card.

You take more and more photos, no longer constrained by the short rolls of film of analog photography.

Your home PC is quickly overwhelmed, the harddrive swamped. What do we do?

Luckily, external harddrives are just catching on. You get a 250GB external hard disk and move the photos over to it. Safe and sound.

This serves you well for a few years, and in the meantime you get some new devices: a new camera with higher resolution and larger storage capacity, a new laptop with its own burgeoning photo library that you try to keep in sync with the other computers.

Eventually all the available space dries up. Not much choice but to start moving stuff around again. You get a new external harddrive, this one four times the size!

That should last a while.

The years go by. Cameras are upgraded and swapped out. Computers and laptops retired and their contents copied to the external drives for safe keeping.

All the while the photo library grows ever larger and if it was unweildy to start with, it is now completely opaque to all but the camera owner.
